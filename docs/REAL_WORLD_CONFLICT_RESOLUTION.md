# Real-World Conflict Resolution Flow

## Core Decision
Jirani should not begin with mediation. It should begin with a careful report of what happened, then help trusted people decide whether the case is safe for mediation.

The guiding example is the recent Kamba-Somali conflict pattern in Kenya: resource pressure, livestock movement, rumors, retaliation, and attacks across Kitui/Garissa-linked communities. In that kind of conflict, calling people into mediation too early can expose informants, spread rumors, or put elders in danger.

## Who Uses Jirani
- **Community member:** reports a threat, rumor, resource dispute, blocked route, livestock movement, or attack without naming themselves.
- **Trusted elder:** checks whether the report is real, exaggerated, or unsafe to discuss.
- **Peace committee or religious leader:** helps decide whether both sides can meet.
- **Chief/local administration:** handles active violence, threats, or protection needs.
- **OSF/community partner:** supports training, cross-location coordination, documentation, and follow-up when local actors invite them.

## Case Flow

### 1. Report What Happened
A person opens Jirani and records:
- approximate area, not exact home or GPS;
- what happened;
- when it happened;
- whether it is direct observation, rumor, or a message from someone else;
- the immediate risk: attack, retaliation, livestock theft, blocked water, road closure, hate speech, displacement, or property destruction.
- sensitive survivor risk: domestic violence, GBV, sexual violence, defilement, or intimate partner abuse.

The app must not ask for names, phone numbers, ID numbers, photos of faces, or exact homes.

### 2. Triage Before Mediation
The report is sorted into one of three practical paths:

| Situation | Jirani Response |
|---|---|
| Active attack, weapons, killings, arson, revenge calls | Protection first. Do not mediate. Notify trusted elders, peace committee, chief/local administration, and safe partner contacts. |
| Rumor, incitement, unverified claim | Verify first. Mark as unconfirmed until trusted local people check it. |
| Resource, grazing, boundary, water, livestock damage without immediate violence | Elder review. Mediation may be possible after both sides are reachable and calm. |
| Domestic violence or GBV | Survivor safety first. Do not mediate. Do not alert the community. Share only with a survivor-chosen support actor or trained protection focal person. |

### 3. Local Verification
A trusted reviewer checks the report using safe methods:
- compare with another trusted source;
- confirm the approximate area and time window;
- separate fact from rumor;
- avoid exposing the reporter;
- decide whether sharing the report helps or increases danger.

The app records the status as:
- **new report**;
- **needs verification**;
- **confirmed concern**;
- **not safe for mediation**;
- **ready for mediation review**;
- **closed or expired**.

### 4. Protection Path
If people are in danger, Jirani supports only protective coordination:
- warn people away from a danger area through trusted channels;
- preserve a short neutral incident note;
- list who has been notified;
- do not convene both sides;
- keep the report private until local actors decide otherwise.

This is the correct path for an attack, revenge threat, armed group report, burned shops, blocked road with violence, or active retaliation.

### 4B. Survivor-Support Path
Domestic violence and GBV reports are not treated like ordinary community conflict reports. Jirani should:
- keep the record local by default;
- avoid names, exact homes, photos, and phone numbers;
- avoid notifying family members, elders, police, or the alleged abuser without survivor consent unless a child or immediate life-threatening danger requires urgent protection;
- allow private handoff only to a survivor-chosen support person, trained GBV focal person, health provider, legal support provider, or psychosocial support provider;
- never create a broad community alert from a survivor-centered report.

### 5. Mediation Path
Mediation starts only when trusted actors decide:
- both sides can be contacted safely;
- the meeting place is neutral;
- elders or peace actors from both sides accept the process;
- the conflict is about a negotiable issue such as grazing routes, water access, livestock damage, market access, boundary use, apology, compensation, or safe return;
- there is no active attack or revenge mobilization.

The mediation record uses neutral labels:
- Community A and Community B;
- Elder A and Elder B;
- Report 1, Report 2, Witness 1 if needed.

It should not store personal blame language.

### 6. Agreement
The agreement should be simple and checkable:
- what each side agreed to do;
- temporary grazing or water schedule;
- compensation or repair step, if locally accepted;
- who will check compliance;
- review date;
- what happens if the agreement fails;
- who may safely receive the record.

### 7. Follow-Up
Jirani should help record:
- whether violence reduced;
- whether the water/grazing/route agreement was followed;
- whether new rumors appeared;
- whether another review meeting is needed.

## App Feature Redesign

### Report
The first screen. Captures conflict and threat reports safely.

### Local Review
Built into the report result. Shows whether the case needs protection, verification, or elder review.

### Mediation
Second-stage tool. Used by elders, peace committees, chiefs, religious leaders, OSF/community partners, or accepted neutral actors after review.

### Agreements
Stores neutral community decisions and review dates.

### Translation
Supports simple shared wording across English, Swahili, Somali, Kamba, and future local languages.

### Sync
Only shares sanitized records when local actors decide sharing is safe.

## Demo Story
A Kamba farmer reports that camels entered farms near a market route and people are spreading revenge rumors. Jirani saves the report without names, marks it as rumor/resource conflict, and says mediation is not ready until elders verify facts.

An elder and peace committee member confirm livestock damage but no active attack in that location. They mark the case ready for mediation review. Elders from both communities meet in a neutral place and agree on a temporary grazing route, compensation discussion, and a review date.

If an attack happens during the process, Jirani moves the case back to protection first and stops mediation guidance.
